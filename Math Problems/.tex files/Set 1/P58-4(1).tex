\noindent As shown in the figure below, segments \(BD\) and \(CE\) are bisectors of  \(\angle ABC\) and \(\angle ACB\), respectively. Find the measure of \angle \emph{x}.\footnote{Rakunan High School, Kyoto}\\

\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[scale = 0.4]{P58-4/P58-4(1).png}
\end{center}

\newpage
\thispagestyle{empty}

\begin{center}
\section*{Solution}
\end{center}

\noindent \begin{math}
Answer: 125^\circ
\end{math}\\

\noindent Proof: \(\angle x=\angle A+\angle ABD+\angle ACE=70^\circ+\frac{1}{2}(\angle ABC+\angle ACB)=70^\circ+\frac{1}{2}(180^\circ-70^\circ)=\mathbf{125^\circ}\).
