\noindent In the figure below, \angle \(ABC=40^\circ\), segments \(DB=DC=AC\). Find the measure of \angle \emph{x}.\footnote{Kagoshima Prefecture}\\

\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[scale = 0.4]{P58-2/P58-2(1).png}
\end{center}

\newpage
\thispagestyle{empty}

\begin{center}
\section*{Solution}
\end{center}

\noindent \begin{math}
Answer: 20^\circ
\end{math}\\

\noindent Proof: Since \(DB=DC\), \(\angle DCB=\angle DBC=40^\circ\). \(\angle ADC=\angle DCB+\angle DBC=40^\circ+40^\circ=80^\circ\). Also, since \(DC=AC\), \(\angle A=\angle ADC=80^\circ\). Therefore, \(\mathbf{\angle x=180^\circ-80^\circ-80^\circ=20^\circ}\).
