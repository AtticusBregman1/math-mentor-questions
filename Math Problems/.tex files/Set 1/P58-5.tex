\noindent In the figure below, two congruent squares are stacked on top of each other. The overlapped sections of the squares create regular octagon \(ABCDEFGH\), with side length 4cm. Using this information, answer the questions below.\footnote{Mie Prefecture}\\
\newline
\noindent (1): Find the measure of an interior angle in octagon \(ABCDEFGH\).\\
\noindent (2): Find the total area of the 8 triangles surrounding octagon \(ABCDEFGH\).\\
\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[scale = 0.4]{P58-5/P58-5.png}
\end{center}

\newpage
\thispagestyle{empty}

\begin{center}
\section*{Solution}
\end{center}

\noindent \begin{math}
Answer: (1): 135^\circ,(2):32cm^2
\end{math}\\

\noindent Proof (1): An exterior angle of a regular octagon is \(360^\circ\div8=45^\circ\). Therefore, the measure of each interior angle is \(\mathbf{180^\circ-45^\circ=135^\circ}\).\\

\noindent Proof (2): The area of an isosceles right triangle is a quarter of the square of the hypotenuse. To calculate the total area of the 8 isosceles right triangles, you can multiply that result by 8: \(\mathbf{(\frac{1}{4}\times4^2)\times8=32cm^2}\).
