Use the quadratic formula to solve $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$.

Recall: For $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, we have $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$.

- [ ] $x = 2$ or $x = 4$
- [x] $x = 2$ or $x = 3$
- [ ] $x = 1$ or $x = 6$
- [ ] $x = -2$ or $x = -3$

---
**Solution:** Here $a = 1$, $b = -5$, $c = 6$. The discriminant is $b^2 - 4ac = 25 - 24 = 1$, so
$$x = \frac{5 \pm 1}{2} = 3 \text{ or } 2$$
